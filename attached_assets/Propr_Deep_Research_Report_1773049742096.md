## Business Model & Economics

### Revenue Streams

Propr's business model has two primary revenue streams, consistent with the broader prop firm industry:

1. **Evaluation Fees (Primary):** Traders pay a one-time fee to attempt a challenge. Across the industry, 70% of prop firm revenue comes from evaluation fees rather than actual trading profits. Challenge fees typically range from $50 to several hundred dollars depending on account size. Propr's specific pricing has not been publicly disclosed yet.

2. **Profit Retention (20% of trader profits):** Propr retains 20% of all profits generated on funded accounts.

3. **Token Economics:** Revenue from pre-TGE operations will feed into a buyback pool. Pre-TGE revenue will fill a buyback pool for $PROPR at launch.

### Unit Economics & Industry Context

The prop firm business model is structurally favorable to the firm. Industry data shows:
- FPFX Tech analysed 300,000+ prop accounts from 100,000 traders across 10 firms: only 14% passed a challenge, 7% of all traders ever reached a payout.
- Only 1–3% of all applicants become long-term, consistently funded traders.

This means the vast majority of challenge fees are pure revenue — most traders fail and either re-purchase or move on. However, Propr's "no time limit" policy could potentially increase pass rates and extend the period before a trader either passes or fails, which has business model implications — as one industry source notes, "unlimited time" and "unlimited scaling" sound appealing but can be "business killers."

### Structural Advantages

- **On-chain execution** reduces counterparty risk concerns that plague many prop firms (many operate B-Book models where the firm profits when traders lose)
- **Hyperliquid infrastructure** provides deep liquidity without Propr needing to build its own order book
- **Lower overhead** compared to firms that build proprietary exchange infrastructure from scratch

### Structural Disadvantages

- **80/20 profit split is at the lower end** of industry standards — competitors like FundedNext offer up to 95%, and Breakout (Kraken) offers up to 90%
- **Max account size of $100K** is below industry standard ($200K is common, with scaling up to $1M+ at leading firms)
- **Dependency on Hyperliquid** means performance and uptime are outside Propr's control

- **Significant.** The prop firm industry is under increasing regulatory scrutiny. The prop trading industry is navigating regulatory changes as agencies like the CFTC, SEC, and IRS reshape operational frameworks.
