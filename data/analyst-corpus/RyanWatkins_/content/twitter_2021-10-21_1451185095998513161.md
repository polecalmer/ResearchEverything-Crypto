### Tweet 3/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185107549626368)

Insights from Macro below.

After seeing metrics fall across the board following the May market crash and subsequent slow down, activity picked up substantially as we reached the end of Q3 2021. 

By the end of the quarter Compound reached new all-time highs across most KPIs. https://t.co/NghHjTk184

---

### Tweet 4/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185116391170060)

Outstanding loans grew 57% in Q3.

A key driver of this growth was the increase in Compound markets liquidity since Q1, which drove interest rates down to their lowest levels in history. 

However as utilization increased in USDC and DAI markets, rates began rising by Q3 end. https://t.co/SZ2LQYtsl7

---

### Tweet 5/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185124347834379)

Between rising collateral values, more favorable market conditions, and attractive borrowing costs, borrowers returned in droves in Q3. 

Unsurprisingly, most of this borrowing activity was denominated in USDC and DAI. https://t.co/ahytr44OJM

---

### Tweet 6/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185132195377165)

Outstanding deposits grew 48% in Q3, driven by rising token prices, as well as fresh deposits chasing higher yields as activity picked up. 

WETH, USDC, and DAI contributed most to growth in Q3. https://t.co/jbn9fTrAdr

---

### Tweet 7/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185139694784514)

Depositors are now receiving the least amount of interest they’ve received in Compound’s history as Compound markets liquidity builds. 

Although the story is nuanced across individual markets, with stablecoin yields having picked up materially in Q3 after reaching lows in July. https://t.co/e1sExgGT8J

---

### Tweet 8/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185146749603858)

This trend of depositor growth outpacing loan growth is perhaps best captured by Compound’s aggregate utilization ratio. 

Since the beginning of 2021 utilization fell 20% from from 58% to 38% as depositors flooded Compound markets with liquidity. https://t.co/ChusjHLZEx

---

### Tweet 9/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185153129156613)

Historically low interest rates also caused Compound to have its first quarter in over a year with lower interest income than the prior quarter. 

Despite loans growing 57% in the quarter, interest income declined 19% from $96 million in Q2 to $78 million in Q3. https://t.co/LeAIycX6i7

---

### Tweet 10/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185160716636160)

This largely reflected lower interest rates charged on DAI and USDC loans for most of the quarter.

Similarly, due to lower borrowing rates, protocol income (reserve factor) saw its first down quarter in over a year falling 17% from $11 million to $9 million. https://t.co/XeRLIbnkfz

---

### Tweet 11/17
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2021-10-21 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1451185167771398148)

Unsurprisingly, liquidations decreased substantially in the quarter, following Q2’s extreme market volatility.

Considering market volatility in the 1st half of Q3 was relatively muted, with the 2nd half of Q3 being fairly one-directional (up), its unsurprising liquidations fell. https://t.co/Vqb0ePLHgl
