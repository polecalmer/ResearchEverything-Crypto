### Tweet 5/10
**@RyanWatkins_** | 2022-01-16 | [link](https://x.com/RyanWatkins_/status/1482853478292762627)

Web 3 gaming economies are far different. 

1) Free floating currencies

2) Free capital flow

3) Free exchange - users can transact among each other, with the publisher, and engage in either side of a transaction (buying or selling).

It’s a free market and open economy.

Combined you have a system where capital flows in at a fixed exchange rate, accrues solely to the publisher, and can never leave the system.

It’s a closed economy where the publisher effectively just issues you a stablecoin you can never redeem and can only use on their platform

So what does this mean?

Web 3 gaming currencies are subject to market forces not even remotely comparable to Web 2 games. 

Their job to hold and accrue real value is far more difficult, and much more akin to building a currency for a new country in the real world.
