Digital Assets Market Ruins Weekends As if the weeks haven’t been long enough, the digital assets market has also been determined to ruin weekends too. We’ve seen a similar pattern emerge in each week since the bloodbath began on March 12th. Bitcoin has traded sharply down on weekends, only to recover during the week. This is a bit of an odd pattern, and may be indicative of a lack of market makers on weekends, which leaves thinner order books and opens the door for more downside volatility.

Looking much further out than just a few weeks, there are signs that the worst is behind us. Bitcoin had its second largest difficulty adjustment last week (-16%). Network difficulty can be defined as a target value that shows how hard it is for miners to solve the hash that would create the next valid block in the blockchain, and its downward adjustment suggests that miners have capitulated (exited the mining competition) as overall network hash decreased and blocks were taking more than 10 minutes on average to be mined. The adjustment maintains the 10 minute block time at the new lower network hash rate. Historically, downward difficulty adjustments of this velocity indicate miner capitulation and market bottoms. We will see how this plays out, but this is a normal facet of Bitcoin's homeostasis, which maintains an natural equilibrium on the blockchain. Said another way, it’s refreshing to see that at least one financial system in the world is designed to self-regulate and auto-adjust without massive intervention. Speaking of intervention…
